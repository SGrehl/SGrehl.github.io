What is Sociology?

Understanding Society and Human Behavior

The scientific study of:

  • Human behavior in social context
  • Groups, communities, and societies
  • Institutions and the structures they create
  • Social problems and their underlying causes

The Sociological Perspective

  • Across all these areas, sociology takes a social, rather than an individual, perspective
  • Focuses not on a certain person, but on groups or society as a whole
  • It seeks structural causes, not individual ones.
  • Aims to reveal underlying patterns in how societies function

Additional Slides

In the Beginning was Philosophy

  • People have always asked questions about society
  • Sociology began as a part of philosophy
  • Early sociologists were moral philosophers

Today: Two Views

View 1: Sociology as a Science

  • Wants to go beyond philosophy (and logical arguments)
  • Strives for mathematical tools, precise data
  • Uses tools like statistics, experiments & simulations
  • Focus on explanations, predictions & intervention

View 2: Sociology as Social Philosophy

  • Wants to stick with philosophy
  • Says: Society is too complex to do otherwise
  • Focus on societal diagnoses
